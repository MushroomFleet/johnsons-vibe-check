## Q0, revisited against execution

The blueprint Q0 classified this as **"a reflection-leaning transposition with real added fuel,"** the fuel being the clean-hands-as-theology hinge (crime → sin) that made it more than *Heat*-in-space. **The prose, as written, does not execute that fuel** — so the executed object sits at a **different point on the derivative/original axis** than the blueprint did:

- The blueprint was *original-via-native-structural-fuel.*
- The prose is a **faithful, superbly-textured transposition** whose originality has **migrated from structure to texture** — from a new tragic *hinge* (theology) to the sealed-world *surface* (the 22-second forage-lock, the *6w* motif, the gray, the counting, the indifferent Star). The structural skeleton has come forward toward *Heat*; the world-texture is now carrying the distinctiveness.

This is not a downgrade in quality — the texture is excellent — but it **changes the answer to "is this more than *Heat* in space?"** The blueprint answered yes via the theology. The prose answers: *yes, via world and restraint* — a quieter, more derivative-leaning claim. Worth naming because it changes what the story *is*.

Tight three-tense alignment holds at the prose level. **The audience-model is sound as executed** — the divergence is not about *who* it serves but about *which tragedy* it delivers to them.

## The central divergence (the diagnosis)

The lorebook's engine and the prose's engine are **different tragedies wearing the same costume:**

- **Lorebook engine — sin summons the law.** A brought-in gun spills *needless* blood "for ease, or appetite, or nothing"; in a clean-hands faith-state this is sacrilege; it brings the law down "like the wrath of heaven," and Vael becomes "an instrument of the Dessein." *Causality: the sin triggers the holy reckoning.*
- **Prose engine — the better hunter closed the gap.** Vael's **off-record reassignment** plants a man on the unwalked gantry *before any blood is spilled*; that guard wounds Crale; Crale kills him **out of necessity**; the killing is the **consequence** of Vael's competence, not the **cause** of his pursuit. *Causality is inverted: the law produces the blood.*

Three structural consequences follow, all visible on the page:

1. **Crale's role is inverted.** Blueprint: the agent of damnation (the gun who sins). Prose: the **object of loyalty** (the loyal hand who watches the gantry, dies, and whom Sevran will not abandon). His function flips from *the thing that damns Sevran* to *the thing Sevran damns himself to keep* — the floor under the code. (Kell and Oster do not appear; the cast is streamlined and Crale absorbs the crew-loyalty weight.)
2. **The theology recedes.** There is little rendering of clean-hands-as-faith, and no moment where blood lands as *sin* rather than as tactical catastrophe. The hinge the blueprint called distinctive is, in these pages, **absent.**
3. **The warmth migrates** (risk #2). With the romance not dramatized anywhere in the finished sequence, the load-bearing warmth becomes **male professional bond** — Crale ("space for two," the passed roll) and Vael ("the truest marriage of his life," the hand held at the end). This is **deeply *Heat*-faithful** (Mann's core is also Hanna↔McCauley and the crew) and arguably *stronger* for this register — but it is a **divergence from the lorebook**, which foregrounded Mira-as-love as the warmth. As executed, **Mira is a sharp ally, not the emotional fulcrum**; the doomed-romance thread the blueprint promised is **not delivered** — and since the sequence is complete, that is a settled fact about the story, not a gap awaiting a page.

**Net:** the prose trades the theological tragedy for the purer *Heat* tragedy — *the code has a floor, and the floor kills him* — and executes that trade **beautifully**. But it is a trade, and it spends the blueprint's distinguishing fuel to make it.

## What the prose does superbly (specific and earned)

This must be said plainly, because the divergence above is a *direction* question, not a *quality* one. The execution is accomplished:

- **Motif discipline** — *6w* as the patient unresolved variable ("the absence of the number, patient as the distance between stars") is a genuinely excellent structural spine; the counting, the gray, the recycled air, the hum you stop hearing all recur without strain.
- **Restraint as characterization** — "you don't outrank me" / "I'm the one dying, that's a rank"; "I didn't ask about the light"; the passed mess-roll *instead of* thanks. The warmth is conveyed by what is withheld. This is the register working exactly as the blueprint hoped ("the warmth exists to measure the loss").
- **Dramatic irony** — the foreknowledge seeded from page 0 ("He does not know yet about the gantry"; "He was wrong about the man. He was right about the floor") makes the doom feel *authored by the world*, not imposed by the writer.
- **The parley handled by indirection** (risk #4) — rather than reproduce *Heat*'s diner set-piece and invite the comparison, the prose lets it live as **memory** and pays it off at the climax ("*Told you*... told you neither of us would hesitate"). Elegant avoidance of the exact comparison risk the blueprint flagged.
- **The ending** executes the fixed final page faithfully and well — the hand over the hand, the kinship as the thing neither man keeps, the indifferent Star, "There was nothing else the living were permitted to do." The non-HOPECORE verdict lands.

- **Complete artifact.** The sequence is the finished redraft (page 2 merged in), so the findings are **settled, not provisional.** The three that a missing page might have softened — the theology receding, the warmth migrating off the romance, the parley living as memory rather than scene — are now **confirmed properties of the finished story.** This removes the main hedge and *strengthens* the diagnosis: the engine swap is the story's actual state, with no reserve page to resolve it.

## The decision this puts in front of the production

The story is currently running **two engines that point at different originality claims**, and the prose has quietly chosen the *Heat*-faithful one. Three coherent ways forward — the worst outcome is drifting between them:

1. **Restore the theology** (recover the blueprint's distinctive fuel). Establish clean-hands-as-faith vividly and early, and stage a killing — perhaps a *second*, genuinely needless one — that lands as **sin**, converting the manhunt into the holy reckoning the lorebook promised. This makes the story more than a transposition again, at the cost of complicating the clean *Heat* engine the prose now runs so well.
2. **Embrace the purer transposition** (ratify what the prose has become). Lean fully into world-texture and restraint as the originality, accept that the distinctiveness is *surface and register* rather than a new hinge — and **update the lorebook to match**, so blueprint and execution agree and the "needless blood / sin / wrath" language stops promising an engine the prose doesn't run.
3. **Relocate the theology to texture** (a middle path). Keep the *Heat*-faithful plot engine, but thread clean-hands-as-faith through the **world's voice and Vael's interiority** so the religious stakes colour the cold without needing a needless killing to trigger them — making the theology *atmosphere* rather than *mechanism*.

Any of the three is viable. The one to avoid is the current implicit state, where the lorebook advertises a theological tragedy and the prose delivers a professional one — because a reader arriving on the lorebook's promise will feel the missing hinge without being able to name it.

---

## Recommendations (craft of address)

1. **Decide the engine on purpose** (the section above) and align lorebook and prose to whichever you choose.
2. **Resolve Mira's role deliberately.** As executed she is competence, not fulcrum, and the romance the blueprint promised is not in the finished text. Either give her a scene that earns the *future-that-won't-be* weight the blueprint assigned her, **or** formally reassign the warmth to the crew/Vael bonds (which are already carrying it well) and update the blueprint so it stops promising a romance the story doesn't run.
3. **Protect the *6w* spine and the indirection of the parley** — these are the executed story's two best structural decisions; whatever engine you choose, keep them.
4. **Keep the threshold signal** that primes the reader for the non-HOPECORE register; it is what converts the wheelhouse departure from break to expansion.
