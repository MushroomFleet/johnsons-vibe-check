# Case Study 01 — *Morbius* (2022): the precisely-aimed phantom

*First off-diagonal test of the Vibe-Check instrument. Companion to `Johnsons-Vibe-Check-grounding.md`; section references (§) point there.*

**Cell under test:** §IV.3 off-diagonal — **a precisely-targeted film that flopped.** This is the expensive half of the experiment; the confirming diagonal (targeted → hit) proves nothing on its own. If the three-question sweep can localise *why* Morbius failed without being fed the outcome, the instrument has earned a point. If it can only explain the flop in hindsight, it has failed §IV.1.

**Claim under test (as proposed):** Morbius was *precisely targeted* yet flopped — and the makers were so confident in the read that they **launched it twice** and watched it flop a second time.

**Headline finding (refines the claim):** The aim was precise; the **lock was a phantom.** Morbius is not "good targeting, bad luck." It is **high-confidence precision aimed at an audience that was no longer in the room** — a §II.1 *phantom model*, not an *early* one. The double-launch is not a footnote; it is the velocity error (§II.2) committed a second time by mistaking **ironic quotation for sincere quotation** (§I.4) — an attempt to harvest a *rehabilitation-product* (§I.12) before any rehabilitation had occurred, or could have.

---

## Case facts (hedged where figures are involved)

- *Morbius* (Sony, dir. Daniel Espinosa, Jared Leto as Michael Morbius), part of **Sony's Spider-Man Universe (SSU)** — the same rights-driven line as the *Venom* films.
- Delayed repeatedly through the pandemic; finally released **April 2022**, ~four months after *Spider-Man: No Way Home* (Dec 2021).
- Critically panned; theatrical performance weak against its budget.
- The **"Morbin' Time" / "Morbius sweep" meme**: a wave of *ironic* online praise — fake quotes, fabricated record-gross claims, sarcastic "greatest film ever" acclaim. Ridicule wearing the costume of praise.
- **The double-launch:** reading the ironic buzz as latent demand, Sony **re-released Morbius to ~1,000+ theatres in June 2022.** It earned a catastrophic per-screen figure — a second, larger humiliation that itself became the punchline.
- **Downstream:** the SSU continued (*Madame Web*, *Kraven the Hunter*, both 2024, both flops) and was then effectively wound down.

---

## Q0 — the slate gate *(asked before the sweep, per §II.6)*

**At what unit was this greenlit, and what does failure cost the slate?**

Morbius is a **portfolio object**: the SSU exists in large part to **exploit and retain Sony's Spider-Man-adjacent character rights**. That raises the legitimate Q0 suspicion of a **rights-hold** or **slate-expansion** play, where a soft opening is priced in.

**But the falsification rent (§II.6) must be paid with a pre-settlement fingerprint — and here the fingerprint points the other way.** A deliberately-priced loss (a *sacrificial anode*, §III/§II.6) is **not re-released.** You do not throw fresh prints-and-advertising money at a loss you intended to take. **The June re-release is the tell that rules the strategic-loss reading OUT:** it is positive evidence of *sincere belief in demand.* Whatever rights-logic sat in the background, the people spending the money in 2022 **thought Morbius could win** — twice. 

**Q0 verdict:** sincere audience-bet, not a priced-in strategic loss. The sweep is therefore licensed (we are reading a film that was *trying to reach* its encoded reader), and the double-launch is admissible as evidence of the makers' actual conviction rather than portfolio theatre.

---

## The three-question sweep *(run blind — from the encoding, per §IV.1)*

Discipline check: every read below is available from choices visible **in the film and its own marketing** — casting, register, post-credits tie-ins, release timing — *not* from the known flop. That is what makes the diagnosis a call the instrument could have lost.

### 1. "Who were they speaking to?" — the present-tense address
Comic-literate, franchise-fluent viewers; the **Venom audience**, primed for a B-tier Spider-villain anti-hero. The post-credits scenes (Vulture / Adrian Toomes, Sinister Six gestures) speak in pure **SSU-insider dialect** — they re-explain nothing and assume total franchise fluency. *Address: the already-initiated devotee.*

### 2. "Who do they think we are?" — the implied present audience (Shadow 1 / **position error**)
They think we are **the 2018 *Venom* audience**: a crowd that will turn out, on brand goodwill and curiosity, for a goofy-dark Spider-villain romp. The instrument that built this model was **calibrated on Venom's surprise success** — a reading of the room that was *true in 2018* and stale by 2022. **Position error:** they think we are where we just were.

### 3. "Who did they think we would become?" — the forecast audience (Shadow 2 / **velocity error**)
The fatal one. *No Way Home* (Dec 2021) had just posted enormous numbers built on **multiverse nostalgia and the beloved legacy Spider-Men.** Sony extrapolated that spike **linearly** into a forecast: *the audience is becoming a Spider-mythology-hungry crowd that will turn out for any Spider-adjacent property.* They drew a straight line through one point. The vector had already curved: *No Way Home*'s heat was about **specific nostalgia objects** (Maguire, Garfield), **not** generic appetite for the Spider-universe. Morbius inherited the extrapolation and none of the actual fuel.

---

## Where the model broke (reading the gaps, §II.5)

The three answers **do not align**, and the sweep localises the fracture at the **forecast tense**:

- The **address** (insider SSU dialect) and the **present-read** (Venom audience) are internally consistent with each other — but both are aimed at a **smaller, already-initiated** audience than a $75M+ tentpole needs.
- The **forecast** (a No-Way-Home-sized general appetite) is *much larger* than the audience the address actually courts.
- **The gap between Shadow 1 and Shadow 2 is the failure:** they built an **insider film** (narrow address) while betting on a **general-appetite turnout** (mass forecast). The film speaks to devotees; the P&L was modelled on a crowd that doesn't speak that dialect. The forecast audience and the addressed audience are **different people**, and the difference is the loss.

This is the **phantom model** (§II.1) precisely: the implied general-appetite audience was **not coherent enough for any real demographic to converge on it.** It wasn't early. There is no future cohort *for this configuration* to grow into, because the configuration was a straight-line projection of a spike that belonged to other films.

---

## The double-launch — a second, rarer diagnostic

The re-release is the case's crown jewel because it is the framework's own machinery **misfired in public**:

- The "Morbin' Time" meme is **ironic quotation** — the *ridicule* phase of §I.4, not the *fondness* phase. Per the grounding doc, ironic quotation can **curdle** into sincere quotation, but that curdling takes **~a decade** and requires **fuel** (§I.6).
- Sony read the **ridicule phase as the fondness phase** — mistook the laughter *at* the film for demand *for* the film — and tried to **manufacture a rehabilitation-product (§I.12) on the spot.**
- This is the velocity error (§II.2) committed a *second* time, now on meme-data instead of box-office-data: another straight line drawn through ironic noise and read as sincere signal.

**The diagnostic value:** the double-launch is hard, behavioural proof of the **lag** at the centre of the whole model (§II.3). The makers' only instrument for reading the present was the recent past (first the Venom/No-Way-Home past, then the just-past meme), and **both readings extrapolated noise as signal in a straight line.** The audience refused to travel in a straight line — twice — and the second refusal was louder than the first.

---

## Bonus shadow — the heraldry misfire (§I.3)

Casting **Jared Leto** imports a specific heraldry: prestige, method-actor intensity, *serious* darkness (Oscar winner; the divisive method Joker). That coat of arms was worn into a film whose actual audience wanted the **Venom register** — goofy, fun, light-dark. **Imported heraldry (prestige intensity) aimed at a reader who wanted its opposite (disposable fun).** A third independent light, and its shadow agrees with the other two: *the film mis-modelled the appetite of the room it was built for.* Triangulation holds (§II.0).

---

## Settlement signal — non-gross (§IV.2)

Gross alone is contaminated (pandemic timing, brand fatigue, dreviews). The cleaner signal per §II.6 is the **producers' own next-bet vector**:

- After Morbius, Sony **did not retreat immediately** — it released *Madame Web* and *Kraven* (2024), then **wound the SSU down.**
- Retreat-after-repeated-attempts is the makers ruling their own model **WRONG, not early.** 
- **Contrast (the control):** Disney *never* retreated from prequel-era material; it mined that vein for a decade because that forecast was settling in its favour — the next-bet vector confirmed *early*. Sony's vector confirms **phantom.**

**Settlement verdict:** the SSU general-appetite audience was a **phantom model**; the producers' own subsequent behaviour adjudicates it as *wrong*, not *premature*.

---

## Forward, falsifiable prediction *(a call the instrument can lose, §IV.1)*

The framework predicts the "Morbin' Time" meme will **fade rather than curdle into sincere fondness**, because §I.6 requires **fuel** — authored coherence — for a spark to catch, and Morbius is a workmanlike, incoherent film with no obsessive vision or tragic spine for the irony to ignite. 

- **Checkable by ~2032:** if a genuine, *sincere* Morbius reappraisal forms (not ironic), the framework is **challenged** — irony curdled without fuel, and the fuel precondition is weaker than claimed. If the meme is simply gone and the film forgotten, the framework is **supported.** 
- Note this is the *same* open question as the sequels (§I.7), tested on a cleaner specimen: **can the cycle rehabilitate something with no authored centre?** Morbius is a near-pure fuel-absence case, so it is a sharp probe of that precondition.

---

## Refinement produced by this case *(the "refine" step of §IV.4)*

Running the case forces a **subdivision of the off-diagonal "targeted-but-flopped" cell** (§IV.3). "Precisely targeted flop" is not one thing:

- **(a) Aimed at a real lock, executionally failed** — the target audience exists and was reachable; the film simply wasn't good/clear enough to turn the key. Rehabilitation is *possible* (fuel may exist).
- **(b) Aimed at a phantom lock** — the targeting was confident and internally precise, but the audience it described **did not exist** at release and has no coherent future form to grow into. Rehabilitation is *structurally unlikely* (no real audience to ever arrive).

**Morbius is a clean (b).** This matters because the two subtypes make **opposite rehabilitation predictions**, so the instrument must classify *which* off-diagonal it's looking at before predicting a film's future. **Proposed grounding-doc amendment:** split the §IV.3 off-diagonal into (a) and (b), and add the test that distinguishes them — *does a real demographic exist that the encoded audience could converge on?* If yes → (a), rehab possible. If no → (b), phantom, rehab unlikely.

---

## Verdict

| Field | Reading |
|---|---|
| **Off-diagonal cell** | Targeted → flopped (the costly, instructive half) |
| **Q0** | Sincere bet, *not* a priced-in strategic loss (the re-release rules that out) |
| **Fracture tense** | **Forecast** (Shadow 2 / velocity), with a Shadow 1 position contribution |
| **Core error** | Insider *address* married to general-appetite *forecast* — the two audiences are different people |
| **Model type** | **Phantom**, not early (no coherent future cohort to grow into) |
| **Double-launch** | Velocity error re-committed; ironic quotation misread as sincere; premature rehabilitation-product |
| **Heraldry** | Leto's prestige-intensity aimed at an audience wanting Venom-fun (third agreeing shadow) |
| **Settlement signal** | Producers' next-bet vector = retreat from the SSU → ruled **wrong**, not premature |
| **Forward prediction** | Meme fades rather than curdles (no fuel); falsifiable by ~2032 |
| **Did the sweep earn its point?** | **Yes** — the insider-address-vs-mass-forecast gap was readable from the encoding (casting, SSU tie-ins, release timing) *before* the flop, so the diagnosis was a call the instrument could have lost. |

**One-line result:** Morbius passes as a valid off-diagonal: the instrument localised the failure to the forecast tense and the phantom-lock subtype **from the artifact's own choices**, not from the grosses — and the case paid rent back to the framework by forcing the (a)/(b) subdivision of the targeted-flop cell.
