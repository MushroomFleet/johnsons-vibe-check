# Johnson's Vibe-Check

A procedure for reconstructing the **encoded audience** of a creative work — the reader/viewer it was actually built for — from the artifact itself, then diagnosing where that audience-model holds or breaks and predicting whether the work will be embraced, rejected-then-rehabilitated, or forgotten.

This skill applies equally to **finished media** (films, shows, franchises) and to **work-in-progress fiction** (lorebooks, story chapters, character rosters), and to **a subject merely being discussed** in the conversation. The full theory lives in `references/`; this file is the operating procedure.

## The one rule of hygiene (never break this)

**Question the encoded demographic, never the maker's values.** The instrument reads *who the work assumes its reader is*, not whether that reader is good or the values are correct. The moment the report adjudicates whether an audience *should* want something, it stops measuring and starts taking sides. Neutrality here is not politeness — it is what keeps the assessment falsifiable and useful. Keep political and moral judgement out of every section.

## Translation: "producers" and "audience" for fiction

The framework was calibrated on film, where "they" = producers and "we" = the audience. When assessing a lorebook, chapter, or unpublished concept, translate cleanly:

- **"They" (the makers)** = the author/worldbuilder and the choices encoded in the text.
- **"We" (the audience)** = the intended reader the text is cut for.
- **"Box office / settlement"** = projected reception and durability — will the intended reader actually exist and converge, and will the work last or age out.

The mechanics are identical; only the vocabulary shifts.

## Before you start

Identify the **subject** and gather it:

- **Uploaded file** (lorebook, chapter, script): read it in full first.
- **In-context subject** (a film/show/character/franchise being discussed, or pasted text): assess from the conversation plus general knowledge. If it is a real released property and current reception/where-it-stands matters, you may search to ground the settlement signal — but the *read itself* must come from the artifact's encoded choices, not from reviews.
- If the subject is ambiguous (which lorebook? which chapter?), ask one clarifying question. Otherwise proceed.

Then read `references/Johnsons-Vibe-Check-grounding.md` for the full definitions of every term used below. Skim `references/Morbius-case-study-off-diagonal.md` and `references/Clerks-case-study-off-diagonal.md` as worked examples — they model the rigor, structure, and honesty expected in the report. `references/Kenobi-Anchor-grounding.md` is the original calibration case, kept for provenance.

## The procedure

Run these in order. Do the read **blind** — reason from the artifact's own choices *before* letting known outcomes (grosses, reviews, fan reaction) leak in. The instrument must make calls it could lose.

### Q0 — the intent gate (ask first)

*At what unit was this made, and what does failure cost?* Establish whether you are reading something built to **win with its intended reader** or something doing another job (portfolio/rights play for media; pure self-expression vs. market-engineered product for fiction). Critically, classify the **method of targeting**:

- **Appeal (engineered, top-down):** the audience modelled from data/trend/market logic. *Phantom-prone* — precision is no defence against an audience that does not exist.
- **Reflection (mirrored, bottom-up):** the author addresses themselves and their own cohort; precision is a by-product of authenticity. *Phantom-immune* (the lock is always real) but **scale-risky** (the real audience may be a terminal niche).

For a strategic-loss reading (made-to-fail, rights-hold) to be admissible, name a **fingerprint visible before settlement**, or discard it as unfalsifiable.

### The three-question sweep (the core)

Three readings at three tenses of the same encoded reader. **The diagnosis lives in the GAPS between the answers, not the answers themselves.**

1. **"Who were they speaking to?"** — the *present-tense address.* Read from exposition density, register, in-jokes, what is assumed-known vs. explained. Who does the text talk to *right now*?
2. **"Who do they think we are?"** — the *implied present audience* (**Shadow 1 — position error**). Read from revealed choices, not stated intent. Who does the encoding *assume is in the room*? A lag here means the work addresses *who the audience just finished being.*
3. **"Who did they think we would become?"** — the *forecast audience* (**Shadow 2 — velocity error**). What bet does the work place on how the reader will evolve? Forecasts usually extrapolate the recent trend in a *straight line* through an audience that *curves*.

### Read the gaps

- **All three align** → coherent, stable audience-model → disproportionately durable. (Maximum alignment = the three collapse to one point, as in self-reflective work.)
- **They diverge** → locate *which tense* broke. Name the gap precisely (e.g. "insider address married to a mass forecast" = the work speaks to devotees but is modelled on a crowd that does not speak that dialect).

### Lock analysis

- **Real vs. phantom lock:** does a real demographic exist that the encoded audience could converge on? No → phantom (rehabilitation structurally unlikely). Yes → real.
- **If real, leading edge vs. terminal niche:** is the niche a *latent mass* (a frontier the culture is moving toward) or *permanently small*? This is often **unknowable at creation** — say so rather than faking certainty.
- **Derivative vs. original:** is this recombined from already-measured material (data/playbook → derivative, landmark-incapable) or sampled from an unmeasured frontier (reflection → original, landmark-capable)?

### Structural corollaries (include only those that apply)

- **Archetype vs. instantiation** — is the work leaning on a trans-historical slot (durable) or only on contemporary surface (expires with the trend)?
- **Aging-out vs. transmission** — is it built for one cohort (will age out) or does it renew/transmit to the next (durable)? Watch for the "jumping the shark" symptom.
- **Self-defined wheelhouse** — does it establish a renewing contract a fandom can self-select into?
- **Aesthetic heraldry** — what era-minted prestige does it import, and is it legible to the intended reader?
- **Hard inversion / binary error** — does it flip an established expectation as if the source were a clean binary? Inverting *noise* yields a dead spot, not a fresh take.

### Settlement signal & prediction

Prefer a **non-gross** signal: projected reader-convergence, rehabilitation trajectory, generativity (does it spawn more?), or the maker's likely next move. Record a **falsifiable prediction**: will the intended audience exist and converge? Will the work be embraced, rejected-then-rehabilitated (and on what timescale), or forgotten? State confidence and what would prove you wrong.

### Falsification honesty (always include)

Flag the read's exposure to: the **post-hoc trap** (am I fitting the model to a known outcome?), **survivorship** (am I reading endurance backward into design?), and **overdetermination** (how many other variables could explain the outcome?). A read that survives these is an instrument; one that doesn't is a horoscope.

### Recommendations (for work-in-progress only)

If the subject is a lorebook, chapter, or concept being workshopped, end with concrete, hygiene-respecting moves: tighten the address, close a specific gap between the three tenses, decide consciously between appeal and reflection, avoid an identified phantom lock, or shore up archetypal fuel. Advise on *craft of address*, never on whose values should change.

## Report output

ALWAYS write the assessment to a **new, semantically named markdown artifact** in `/mnt/user-data/outputs/` (do not bury it inline). Name it after the subject, e.g. `<Subject>-vibe-check-report.md` (slugify: `Eldoria-lorebook-vibe-check.md`, `Chapter-7-Ashfall-vibe-check.md`, `Blade-Runner-2049-vibe-check.md`). Then present it with `present_files`.

Use this structure (adapt headings to fit the subject; drop sections that don't apply):

```markdown
# Vibe-Check — <Subject>

*One-line statement of what was assessed and the headline finding.*

## Subject & scope
## Q0 — intent gate            (unit; appeal vs reflection; strategic-loss check)
## The three-question sweep    (the three tenses, read blind)
## Reading the gaps            (where the model holds or breaks, and at which tense)
## Lock analysis               (real vs phantom; leading-edge vs terminal; derivative vs original)
## Structural corollaries      (only those that apply)
## Settlement & prediction     (non-gross signal; falsifiable call + confidence)
## Falsification honesty       (post-hoc / survivorship / overdetermination exposure)
## Recommendations             (work-in-progress only; craft of address, never values)
## Verdict                     (compact table: cell / mechanism / prediction)
```

Match the analytical rigor and intellectual honesty of the worked case studies in `references/`: concede uncertainty, prefer the sharp finding over the flattering one, and keep every section inside the hygiene rule.

## Quality bar

A good Vibe-Check report (a) reads the audience from the artifact's own choices, not from reviews or the maker's statements; (b) locates the diagnosis in the *gaps* between the three tenses; (c) classifies the lock as real or phantom and the method as appeal or reflection; (d) makes a falsifiable prediction with stated confidence; and (e) stays politically neutral throughout, questioning only the encoded demographic. If a report skips the gaps, asserts an outcome without a blind read, or drifts into judging values, it has failed the bar.
