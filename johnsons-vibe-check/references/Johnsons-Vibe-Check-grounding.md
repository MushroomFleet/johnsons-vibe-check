# Johnson's Vibe-Check — Grounding Document

*A unified theory and a portable procedure for reading the **encoded audience** of a blockbuster production: who a work was built for, reconstructed from the work itself rather than from what its makers said about it.*

**Status:** Unified grounding. Supersedes and absorbs *Kenobi-Anchor-grounding.md*. Part I is the **substrate** (the rehabilitation cycle, calibrated on Obi-Wan). Part II is the **instrument** (the shadow method and the three-question sweep). Part III is the **structural corollaries** (survival, aging-out, transmission). Part IV is the **falsification discipline** without which the instrument becomes astrology. Everything is drawn from public cultural knowledge; no proprietary source material is required.

**Calibration case:** Obi-Wan Kenobi (Alec Guinness, 1977 → Ewan McGregor, 1999–2005, 2022).

**The instrument's one rule of hygiene:** *We do not question them. We question the demographic they encoded.* The makers' values are never the object. Their **model of the reader** is the object. This restriction is not politeness; it is what keeps the instrument falsifiable rather than factional.

# PART I — THE SUBSTRATE: the rehabilitation cycle

## I.1 The core claim

> A character instantiation functions as a **cultural landmark** — a fixed marker of what a culture valued at the moment of its making. The audience's "love" for it is less love of the character than **recognition of the landmark as *theirs*.**

- Rehabilitation is **generational, not critical.** The prequels did not get better; the cohort that imprinted on them as children aged into cultural power and now defends its own landmark.
- The character is the **surveyor's pin.** The affection is **territorial.**

## I.2 Archetype vs. instantiation (load-bearing distinction)

- **Archetype** — trans-historical, the structural slot. The Wise Mentor: Merlin, Gandalf, Obi-Wan. The slot is constant.
- **Instantiation** — the time-stamped filling of that slot.
- **What dates is the moral valence the era assigns the slot**, not the slot itself. 1977: Obi-Wan earnest, mythic, sacrificial. 2017: Luke as the mentor who *failed* — only possible after two decades of cultural suspicion of heroes. Same slot, opposite charge. **Archetype = constant; grounding = variable.**

## I.3 Aesthetic heraldry

**Definition:** the prestige an actor or production imports *for free* from the established cinematic moment it belongs to — read by the audience before a word is spoken, like a coat of arms earned elsewhere and worn into the frame. **Heraldry is era-minted**; two vintages can be equal currency while looking nothing alike.

- **Guinness — postwar British prestige.** Ealing, Lean epics, RADA diction, knighthood (1959), Oscar (*Bridge on the River Kwai*). Authority via *diction and stillness*. His private disdain ("fairy-tale rubbish") lent the Mentor effortless weight he didn't have to act for.
- **McGregor (1999) — post-*Trainspotting*, Cool-Britannia prestige.** Grime, kinetic energy, working-class cool. Authority via *dirt rather than diction*. The 1999 audience couldn't read it as heraldry because it looked like the *absence* of Guinness's kind. It took ~20 years for the culture to recognise that grimier / faster / more wounded **is its own dignity**.
- **Mutual unreadability:** each heraldry is *illegible* in the other's era. *McGregor didn't change; the 2022 audience finally had the lens.*

## I.4 The rehabilitation arc and the meme pathway

The prequel Obi-Wan was mass-mocked for ~10 years. The meme-bait lines ("Hello there," "You were the chosen one!", "I have the high ground") crossed the threshold where **ironic quotation curdles into sincere quotation** and the two became indistinguishable. **The meme *was* the rehabilitation mechanism.** Post-nineties canonisation pathway: *you don't get knighted by meme, but you do get a Disney+ show.*

## I.5 The causal engine: rehabilitation by contrast (Abe Simpson)

Exact quote: *"I used to be with 'it.' Then they changed what 'it' was. Now what I'm with isn't 'it,' and what's 'it' seems weird and scary to me. And it'll happen to you."* The joke is that **'it' is empty** — pure position, no content.

- A rejected work is **not** redeemed by aging into nostalgia. It is redeemed the instant a **worse successor** arrives and gives it something to be better than. Affection is **relational** — computed against the next term.
- **Proof in the VHS detail:** there was no "original trilogy" until 1999. The prequels **created** the originals as a love object. Rejection-of-the-new and rehabilitation-of-the-old are **the same event seen from two ends.**
- **Strong form:** the role is a **position in a moving series**; every term occupies each position once — *scary-new-thing* on arrival, *beloved-old-thing* when the next lands.
- **Gatekeepers, not audiences, reject.** 1977's critics who called Star Wars nonsense were the prior cohort defending New Hollywood (*Godfather*, *Chinatown*, *Taxi Driver*). Audiences adored it instantly. The gatekeepers are always the prior cohort.

## I.6 Spark and fuel

Pure contrast can't be the whole story, or nothing would stay buried (the *Holiday Special* has had 40+ years of worse successors and never returned). Therefore:

- **Successor = spark** (ignites). **Authored coherence = fuel** (what catches).
- The prequels had fuel: obsessive single vision, a real tragic spine. The *Holiday Special* has none; the spark finds nothing to burn.

## I.7 The cyclical question (sequels)

By pure cohort-succession mechanics the sequels *should* be rehabilitated by ~2035 (the 2015 child-cohort aging into cultural power). **The asymmetry that may break it:** the prequels had an authored centre to grow into; the sequels may not (three films pulling against each other, no agreed payoff). **Falsifiable fork:** loved anyway → affection is *purely* generational; *not* loved → the machine **requires authored fuel** as a precondition.

## I.8 The terminal problem

If a thing is only ever rehabilitated by a *worse successor*, the cycle saves term **n−1** but never **n**. The newest term is structurally **waiting for a victim.** "Will the sequels be loved?" becomes "will something worse arrive to make them look good?"

## I.9 The barb: "and it'll happen to you"

Knowing the mechanism does not exempt you from it. The machine outputs **affect, not judgment.** You cannot argue yourself into nostalgia on schedule. *This is why the law is strong enough to build on: it survives being known.*

## I.10 Eliot corollary (tradition runs backward)

A powerful new work **rearranges how we read everything before it** ("Tradition and the Individual Talent"). After the prequels, Vader's first entrance becomes the back half of a tragedy; Guinness's stillness re-reads as a man who has buried something. **The retelling reaches backward and edits the original.**

## I.11 Two senses of "betrayal" (kept distinct)

- **Betrayal as plot content** — Obi-Wan reframed as the man who failed his student. Makes the character **sadder and deeper over time.**
- **Betrayal as audience relationship** — "this isn't my Obi-Wan." **Resolves and warms over time.**
- The franchise **weaponises the first to cure the second.**

## I.12 The capstone: *Kenobi* (2022) as proof-of-canonisation

The Disney+ series is **the empirical event that proves rehabilitation completed** — the metric ("you do get a Disney+ show") made literal. It marks the fourth cycle position, *landmark re-erected on demand*, and arrives **during sequel-disappointment** — the culture flinching back toward a trusted pin because the new pins failed to set. It forces the **asserted-vs-earned** fork: the prequels *earned* McGregor's gravitas over 20 years; the show *asserts* it from frame one. Best understood as a **rehabilitation-product, not a rehabilitation-mechanism** — commissioned *because* the climb finished, then feeding on it.

# PART II — THE INSTRUMENT: reading the encoded audience

## II.0 The shadow method

The object — the producers' true model of the audience — is obscured by **bright light**: the stated intention (press kit, interviews, marketing's account of itself). That glare is unreliable; testimony is performance. But the object **casts a shadow** — the **revealed model**, the choices baked into the cut that nobody defends in an interview because nobody made them as conscious claims. **Revealed preference beats stated preference**, for the same reason it does in economics: the testimony is performance, the artifact is confession.

**Read the model from what they couldn't help encoding, never from what they said they were doing.**

**The underdetermination hazard:** a sphere and a disc cast the same circular shadow. One shadow is not enough. **Triangulate** across independent lights:
- **Exposition density** → assumed prior knowledge.
- **Moral legibility** (how brightly good/evil are signposted) → assumed appetite for ambiguity.
- **Merchandising surface** (what's built to be sold) → who's assumed to be buying.
- **Runtime & cutting rhythm** → assumed attention span.
- **Localization flatness** → assumed *global* legibility.

Where the shadows agree, the object is real. Where they contradict, you've found **a production at war with its own audience-model** — itself the most diagnostic finding available.

## II.1 Shadow 1 — "Who do they think we are?" (the position error)

The implied **present** audience. The instrument that builds it is calibrated on the **departed cycle**, so it describes an audience slightly *behind* the one in the room. The initial rejection is the moment the implied-audience model is revealed **mismatched to the actual audience present.** Rehabilitation = a later actual audience arriving that **matches the audience the work was built for.** The producers weren't wrong; they were **early.** *The implied audience is a forecast that comes true late.*

- **Early model** = forecast that eventually materialises (prequels). **Phantom model** = forecast that never materialises (*Holiday Special*). The difference is whether the implied audience was *coherent enough to grow into* — i.e. the **fuel** of §I.6.

## II.2 Shadow 2 — "Who do they think we will become?" (the velocity error)

The **forecast** audience: a deliberate bet with a settlement date. Unlike Shadow 1's accidental lag, this is willed — and a placed bet **generates a verdict.** They take the audience's recent trend and extrapolate it **linearly forward**, but the audience-vector **curves.**

- **2016 *Ghostbusters*** bet that subversion-appetite would keep accelerating — a straight line through an audience already curving toward restoration.
- **Subversion is always a Shadow-2 bet** ("you've *become* sophisticated enough to prefer the inversion"). Repeated to extremes, inversion becomes the convention and **sincere restoration becomes the actual subversion.**

## II.3 The derivative unification

The two shadows are the **zeroth and first derivatives of one lagged instrument.**
- Shadow 1 = error of **position**: *they think we are where we just were.*
- Shadow 2 = error of **velocity**: *they think we'll become more of who we just were, faster.*
Both have the identical root — **the only instrument for seeing the audience is the recently departed past** — which produces a position error and a velocity error simultaneously. The forward-address is **an artifact of the lag, not a noble intention.** *They think we are who we just finished being; the miss we feel on opening night is the audience refusing to travel in a straight line.*

## II.4 Hard inversion and the binary error (the Hemsworth specimen)

A **hard inversion** treats the original as a **clean binary** and flips every bit (competent→ditzy, scripted→ad-libbed). The flip *presupposes* the source was binary. But characters with texture are **noise, not bits** — and **noise has no negation.** Invert a "0" and you get a legible "1"; invert *noise* and you get the **dead spot** — an absence papered over with "ad-libbed," which is the production confessing in its own paperwork that no one wrote the part.

> **Hard inversion fails because it assumes a clean binary that abstraction, nuance, and noise make false.** It is a category error about the substrate, not a political error. (Proof: the performers' talent and range are intact elsewhere — the variable wasn't them. Routing the failure through "female-led" mistakes the surface for the mechanism.)

## II.5 The three-question sweep *(the pivotal procedure)*

> The procedure, and the order is not arbitrary — it's a **temporal sweep across the artifact**, three readings at **three tenses of the same encoded reader**:
>
> - **"Who were they speaking to?"** — the **present-tense address.** Read from exposition density, register, in-jokes, what's assumed-known versus explained. *The reader the text talks to right now.*
> - **"Who do they think we are?"** — the **implied present audience**, read from revealed choices not stated intent (Shadow 1, the position error). *Who the encoding assumes is in the room.*
> - **"Who did they think we would become?"** — the **forecast audience**, the bet with a settlement date (Shadow 2, the velocity error). *Who they extrapolated us into.*

**The diagnostic payload is in the GAPS between the three answers, not the answers themselves.**
- **All three align** → a coherent production with a stable audience-model. *Hypothesis: these are disproportionately the durable, successful ones*, because internal coherence is what lets a work travel to its forecast cohort intact.
- **They diverge** → the sweep localises *at which tense* the model broke (2016 Ghostbusters: fine address, plausible present-read, fatal forecast). Resolution that "it flopped" does not provide.

## II.6 Q0 — the slate gate *(must be asked BEFORE the sweep)*

The sweep assumes the film was **trying to reach** its encoded reader. Not always true. The unit of intention is **not the film — it is the slate** (a portfolio). A portfolio manager does not need every position to win; the *book* must net out. So a loss can be **fully priced at greenlight** and serve the slate:

- **Rights-hold** — release to keep a brand's rights from lapsing.
- **Trial balloon** — buy real market data on an audience-hypothesis for one write-down.
- **Talent-bind / output-deal** satisfaction.
- **Sacrificial anode** — corrode the baseline so a legacy heir clears a low bar.

> **Q0 — At what unit was this greenlit, and what does it cost the slate if it fails?**

Read a sacrificial anode as a sincere audience-bet and you misdiagnose every shadow it casts. "Making movies is about taking risks" (Lucas) is the public face of risk-as-instrument: a portfolio taking no losses is taking no risks.

**Falsification rent (mandatory):** a strategic-loss reading is admissible **only if it leaves a fingerprint visible *before* settlement** — suspiciously contained budget (anode pricing), rights lapsing without release (rights-hold), a pivot too fast to be reactive (pre-loaded correction), a controversy-magnet dropped into a culture-war slot (data-buy). *"Secretly meant to fail" with no pre-settlement fingerprint is unfalsifiable and inadmissible.* The portfolio frame is the most powerful tool here **and** the most dangerous, because it can be retro-fitted to any outcome; the rent is what stops it becoming a conspiracy theory.

## II.7 What this is, beyond character authenticity

Authenticity asks whether a character is true **to itself** (construction). This instrument asks whether a character is cut for a **targeted reader fixed at the workshopping stage** (address). A character is a **precisely-cut key for a specific demographic lock.** The two tests are independent: a character can be flawlessly authentic and still miss, because it was aimed at a reader who doesn't exist. Hemsworth's secretary fails both — *authenticity:* no one wrote the part; *address:* the part was aimed at no one — but they are different failures, and keeping them distinct is the advance.

## II.8 The two ways to cut the key — appeal vs. reflection *(established by Cases 01 & 02)*

A well-cut key can be produced by **two different mechanisms with opposite risk profiles.** Both live in the original thesis ("precise appeal **or** reflection to its intended reader"); the matched pair *Morbius* / *Clerks* carves the thesis at that joint.

- **Appeal (calculation, top-down).** Engineer the audience from data, model the demographic, construct precise appeal. **Failure mode: the phantom lock.** You can calculate your way to an audience that does not exist (*Morbius*, §II.1).
- **Reflection (mirror, bottom-up).** Address yourself and your own cohort; precision is a *by-product* of authenticity. **Structurally phantom-immune** — you cannot aim at a non-existent version of yourself, so the lock is *always real* (*Clerks*).

**But phantom-immune ≠ safe.** Reflection guarantees the lock is **real**; it guarantees nothing about whether the lock is **big.** Its risk is the **terminal niche** — a real audience that stays permanently small. So the two risks are **asymmetric, and the felt risk is inverted from the true risk:**

| | Appeal / data | Reflection / self |
|---|---|---|
| The risk | Lock may be a **phantom** (not exist) | Lock may be **terminal** (real but small) |
| How the risk *feels* | **Safe** — the numbers say the audience is there | **Reckless** — no numbers, just the self |
| True profile | Phantom-prone; landmark-incapable | High-variance; landmark-capable |

*Data-betting feels safe and is secretly phantom-prone; self-betting feels reckless and is the only path to an original.* "Nobody had any idea how strongly it would resonate" is the exact phenomenology of a high-variance reflection bet whose payoff is unknowable when placed.

## II.9 Why data cannot build landmarks — the leading-edge mechanism *(keystone)*

This is the deepest consequence of the lag (§II.3), now stated as a property of the *method* rather than an accident of any one film.

- **Algorithms, data-scraping, and playbooks are the industrialised form of the lag.** Data exists only about the **measured past**; a playbook is a record of what worked **before.** The method is therefore *constitutionally* backward-looking — blind to any demographic that has not yet generated data, which is exactly where new landmarks are born.
- Such a method can **recombine** the measured past into **derivatives**, but is **incapable of an original**, because an original requires sampling the **unmeasured frontier.**
- The only instrument that reads the unmeasured frontier is a **maker reflecting a leading edge from the inside.** Such a maker is a **living sample** of an emergent shape, so rendering the self faithfully renders the *coming mainstream* — **prediction by embodiment, not extrapolation.** (Smith did not forecast the next decade; he was an early *specimen* of it.)

> **A genuinely new cultural landmark cannot be manufactured by data, ever — because data is the fossil record of *old* landmarks. Data builds derivatives; only reflection-of-a-frontier builds originals — and that is unavoidably a gamble, because you cannot know in advance whether your frontier is a leading edge or a dead end.**

**The closing irony of the pair:** *Morbius* is a data-driven **derivative** of the very mainstream that *Clerks* helped **call into being** by reflection. The comic-shop frontier Smith sampled in 1994 became the comic-book-movie monoculture an algorithm later strip-mined so mechanically it produced a phantom. *The frontier became the water; the playbook drowned in it.*

**Full causal chain for a landmark** (three independent conditions, only one controllable):
**authentic reflection** (real lock — *controllable*) × **leading-edge niche** (latent mass — ***unknowable at creation***) × **distribution** (access, e.g. a Sundance/Miramax gate — *separate*). A maker can *know* they have the first; the second is the bet; the third is luck or muscle. This is the honest resolution of the survivorship problem (§IV.3): the thousands of forgotten authentic indies reflected niches that were **terminal**, not leading edges. Authenticity buys a real lock; it does **not** buy scale.

# PART III — STRUCTURAL COROLLARIES: survival, aging-out, transmission

**The master variable of Part III:** *whether the implied audience renews.* Aging-out, the wheelhouse, and the two survivals are this one variable read at four settings.

## III.1 The transmission vector (resolved second shadow)

"Who do they think we will become?" most often resolves to: **parents.** The forecast about the current audience is **reproductive** — that we age out of *watching* and into being the **delivery mechanism** for the next audience. **Fan service is not for the fans; it is a toll** — addressed to the original cohort to get them to *bless the transmission* (bring the kids, grant passage). The fans are the **vector, not the target.** You **cannot transmit a subversion to a child who never held the original expectation** — inversion is illegible to a first-time viewer — so transmission **demands sincerity** (the white hat worn straight). Subversion is a luxury good sellable only to the already-initiated.

## III.2 Aging-out (Happy Days / "jumping the shark")

A mechanically **endless** format (the sitcom engine) still **ended** — proof that format-endlessness is irrelevant; what dies is the **audience-model.** A show built for one cohort and aged *with* it (rather than transmitting past it) **ages out** when that cohort leaves. **"Jumping the shark" is the visible symptom, not the cause** — the moment the model's strain to hold a departed room becomes watchable. The death rattle made legible. **Aging-out is the exact inverse of the transmission vector:** transmission renews the implied audience; aging-out locks it to one cohort with an expiry date.

## III.3 The self-defined wheelhouse (artists, musicians)

A **cultured/honoured fandom** survives because its implied audience is not a cohort snapshot but a **renewing contract.** The artist and the fandom **co-author a wheelhouse**; while the artist operates inside it, the membrane regenerates — new members self-select *in* because the contract is legible and stable. "Cultured" in the literal sense: **a culture that reproduces itself.** The wheelhouse is the antidote to aging-out — a cohort with an expiry vs. a contract with renewal built in. (Risk: departing the self-defined wheelhouse breaks the contract and triggers the rejection reflex, because the audience was promised *this* membrane.)

## III.4 The sliding window and the two survivals (Casablanca vs. Mary Poppins)

Both clear the **sliding window of cinema history**, "each for different reasons" — and the difference exposes the **two distinct survival mechanisms**:

- **Casablanca — passive survival, on archetypal bedrock.** The perennial-*adult* slot (love, sacrifice, honour under occupation), quotation-dense, **re-read** rather than re-shown. This is **fuel that never burns down** (§I.6) — survival by *not dating.*
- **Mary Poppins — active survival, as a transmission engine.** Literally handed parent-to-child every generation; a perennial **child-induction machine**, structurally the same handoff as a legacy sequel (§III.1) but **stable rather than harvested.** Survival by *re-running benignly.*

> One **floats** (bedrock); the other **swims** (transmission). Durability has (at least) two independent routes, and the three-question sweep should identify which route a survivor is on.

## III.5 Durability tracks the reality of the lock, not the precision of the aim *(load-bearing finding, from Cases 01 & 02)*

The matched off-diagonal pair — *Morbius* (precise aim, phantom lock → flopped, **sterile**) against *Clerks* (no aim, real lock → endured, **generative**) — are **opposite on targeting and opposite on outcome.** The variable that tracks the outcome is therefore **not targeting precision.** It is **the reality of the lock** (real vs. phantom) and, downstream, whether the real lock was a **leading edge** (scale).

- **Generativity is the durability tell.** A phantom-locked product is **sterile** — it cannot sustain successors or careers (the SSU wound down). A real-locked original is **generative** — it spawns a universe and launches talent (the Askewniverse; Affleck, Lee, Adams). *Same shared-universe structure; opposite fate, decided by the reality of the base audience.*
- **Construction quality ≠ address quality** (§II.7), proven by the pair: *Clerks* is sloppy in construction and surgical in address; durability followed **address**, not construction.
- **Corrective to the original thesis:** "surgically constructed with precise appeal" (the *appeal* path) is the more **failure-prone** route — precision is no defence against a phantom. "Reflection to its intended reader" (the *reflection* path) is the **durable** route, because it is immune to both the lag and the phantom lock (§II.8–II.9). *Where the two conflict, authenticity wins.*

# PART IV — FALSIFICATION DISCIPLINE

The study explicitly invites **support *or* challenge, then refinement.** A framework that only confirms is a flattering mirror. Three traps the instrument must survive, or it is a horoscope with footnotes:

## IV.1 The post-hoc trap
Reading the encoded demographic *after* knowing the outcome is near-unfalsifiable — every hit looks surgically targeted, every miss looks like a misread, because the model is fitted to the result. **The honest version reads the three answers *blind* — from internal choices alone, ideally predictively, before settlement — then checks against outcome.** The instrument must make calls it can *lose.*

## IV.2 The overdetermination problem
Box office is the **noisiest** settlement signal — marketing spend, release date, brand fatigue, competition, scandal, culture-war crossfire (which engulfed 2016 as a *symbol* before it was seen as a film). The encoded-demographic read is **one variable in a crowded field.** Prefer a cleaner settlement signal than gross: **rehabilitation trajectory**, or the **producers' own next-bet vector** (which filters specifically for the audience-model question).

## IV.3 The survivorship illusion
"The successful ones align strongly" is exactly what you'd observe **even if alignment were random**, because the misaligned-but-successful and the aligned-but-failed both fall out of view. To show alignment **causes** durability rather than merely accompanying it, **hunt the off-diagonal**: the precisely-targeted film that flopped, and the sloppily-targeted film that endured. *The confirming diagonal is cheap; the off-diagonal is the experiment.*

**The off-diagonal is four cells, not two** *(refined by Cases 01 & 02)*:
- **Targeted → flopped:** (a) aimed at a **real** lock but executionally failed — rehab *possible*; (b) aimed at a **phantom** lock — rehab *structurally unlikely*. ***Morbius* = (b).**
- **Sloppy → endured:** (c) genuinely **lucky-sloppy** — survived on noise/distribution; (d) **not sloppy targeting at all** — authentic **self-reflection** that *looks* untargeted because there is no visible engineering. ***Clerks* = (d).**

**The distinguishing test:** *does a real demographic exist that the encoded audience could converge on?* Yes → (a) or a real lock; No → (b) phantom. And: *was the targeting engineered (appeal) or reflected (mirror)?* — separating (c) from (d).

**Worked cases on file:** `Morbius-case-study-off-diagonal.md` (cell b), `Clerks-case-study-off-diagonal.md` (cell d). Together they establish §III.5.

**Still open — the off-*off*-diagonal:** the **authentically self-expressed film that did *not* endure** (a *terminal-niche* reflection). This is the cell that tests whether authentic address is *sufficient* for durability or merely *necessary* — i.e. whether distribution and the leading-edge condition (§II.9) are doing the decisive work. Until it is populated, *Clerks* shows **consistency** with the framework, not **confirmation.**

## IV.4 The experimental loop
1. Pick a case. **Q0** first (slate unit; priced-loss fingerprints).
2. Run the **three-question sweep** blind.
3. Read the **gaps**; record a **prediction** (early vs. wrong vs. phantom; will/won't rehabilitate).
4. Check against a **non-gross settlement signal.**
5. **Support / challenge / refine** the procedure. Repeat.

## Glossary of coined terms

- **Encoded / implied audience** — the reader a work was built for, reconstructable from the artifact itself.
- **The hygiene rule** — *question the encoded demographic, never the makers' values.* Keeps the instrument falsifiable, not factional.
- **Aesthetic heraldry** — era-minted prestige imported for free into the frame (§I.3).
- **Landmark / pin** — a character instantiation as a fixed cultural marker; love of it is territorial (§I.1).
- **Cohort succession** — rehabilitation driven by the imprinted childhood cohort aging into cultural power (§I.1, §I.7).
- **Contrast engine** — affection computed relationally against a worse successor (§I.5).
- **Spark and fuel** — successor (spark) ignites authored coherence (fuel); contrast can't create love from nothing (§I.6).
- **The terminal problem** — the cycle saves n−1 but never n; the newest term waits for a victim (§I.8).
- **The barb** — knowledge of the mechanism does not exempt one from its affect (§I.9).
- **Capstone / rehabilitation-product** — an object commissioned *because* rehabilitation already completed; a *product* of the cycle, not a *stage* in it (§I.12).
- **Asserted vs. earned gravitas** — prestige granted by scale + prior canonisation vs. won slowly via the meme-pathway (§I.12).
- **The shadow method** — read the revealed model from encoded choices, not stated intent; triangulate multiple lights (§II.0).
- **Position error (Shadow 1)** — *they think we are where we just were* (§II.1).
- **Velocity error (Shadow 2)** — *they think we'll become more of who we just were, faster* (§II.2).
- **Early vs. phantom model** — an implied audience that eventually materialises vs. one that never will (§II.1).
- **Hard inversion / the binary error** — flipping a source that was noise, not bits; noise has no negation (§II.4).
- **The three-question sweep** — the present/present-implied/forecast tenses of the encoded reader; diagnosis lives in the gaps (§II.5).
- **Q0 / the slate gate** — the unit of intention is the portfolio; losses can be priced-in; admissible only with a pre-settlement fingerprint (§II.6).
- **Address vs. construction** — this instrument tests the character-as-key-for-a-demographic-lock; authenticity tests the character's truth to itself. Independent (§II.7).
- **Appeal vs. reflection** — two ways to cut the key: engineered top-down (appeal, phantom-prone) vs. mirrored bottom-up (reflection, phantom-immune but scale-risky) (§II.8).
- **The risk inversion** — data-betting *feels* safe and is phantom-prone; self-betting *feels* reckless and is the only path to an original (§II.8).
- **Terminal niche vs. leading edge** — a reflected real lock that stays permanently small vs. one that is a latent mass; the difference is *unknowable at creation* (§II.9).
- **Prediction by embodiment** — a maker on a leading edge samples the coming mainstream by reflecting the self, not by extrapolating data (§II.9).
- **Data builds derivatives, reflection builds originals** — landmarks cannot be manufactured from data, which is the fossil record of old landmarks (§II.9 keystone).
- **Landmark causal chain** — authentic reflection (controllable) × leading-edge niche (unknowable) × distribution (separate gate) (§II.9).
- **Generativity** — the durability tell: real-locked originals spawn universes and careers; phantom-locked products are sterile (§III.5).
- **Transmission vector** — the audience modelled as the *delivery mechanism* for the next audience; fan service as toll (§III.1).
- **Aging-out** — an implied audience locked to one cohort, dying when it leaves; "jumping the shark" is its visible symptom (§III.2).
- **The self-defined wheelhouse** — implied audience as a renewing contract co-authored by artist and fandom; a culture that reproduces itself (§III.3).

## Open questions carried into the next phase

1. **Unit of measurement (primary).** Does the pin **hold still** while the culture re-reads it, or does each instantiation **relocate** the pin? Spark-and-fuel burns differently under each.
2. **Authored-fuel precondition.** Is authored coherence strictly *necessary* for rehabilitation, or merely accelerative? The sequels are the live experiment.
3. **Third heraldry.** Is streaming-era asserted-gravitas genuine prestige or the prior two heraldries cashing out?
4. **Terminal redemption.** Is there any rehabilitation pathway for the newest term that does *not* require a worse successor?
5. **Closed vs. open Q0 list.** Are the strategic-loss readings (anode, trial-balloon, rights-hold) a **closed checklist** (more falsifiable) or an **open class** where the analyst must name the specific job and its tell each time (more truthful)? Decides whether Q0 is a checklist or a discipline.
6. **Settlement signal.** What is the cleanest non-gross proxy for "was the demographic well-cut" — rehabilitation trajectory, next-bet vector, or something composite?
7. **Wheelhouse departure.** Can the instrument predict, in advance, when leaving a self-defined wheelhouse will break the contract vs. successfully expand it?
8. **Off-off-diagonal (primary next experiment).** The authentically self-expressed film that did *not* endure (terminal-niche reflection) — needed to test whether authentic address is *sufficient* or merely *necessary* for durability.
9. **Leading-edge predictability.** Is there *any* pre-settlement heuristic for whether a reflected niche is a leading edge or a terminal niche — or is it irreducibly unknowable at creation (§II.9)? If even weakly predictable, the reflection bet stops being pure variance.
10. **Can data ever sample forward?** Is the "data builds only derivatives" claim absolute, or can instrumentation eventually read emergent/unmeasured demographics (e.g. fine-grained early-signal detection) — and if so, does that collapse the appeal/reflection distinction (§II.8–II.9)?
